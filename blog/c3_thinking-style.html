Cultural Blog Week 3

Learning Styles and difficulties with DBC

Today's Date

Unit 1 of Phase 0 is coming to an end. Over the past 3 weeks we've learned how to use Git and Github for version control. We learned the basics of HTML and CSS and have finally started learning Ruby. It has been a challenge to say the least and also one of the most enjoyable and interesting fields I've ever studied. It's interesting because it's almost exactly as I pictured it. Solving coding problems has been more a creative process then a rigid formula to follow. It's creativity expressed through logic with only the bounds of syntax to hold you.

That having been said, it's still extremely challenging. I've been frustrated by problems for hours where I had 30 lines of code and found that the answer can be solved in as little as 6 or 7. I've had eureka moments when things finally do work. I've also had the grind of solving individual issues until the project worked.

I've learned a lot about myself through these challenges. For one thing I'm a experimenter, a tinkerer. According to the Gregorc thinking style I'm a "Concrete Random Thinker." This means I like to experiment to find answers, I take risks, I rely on intuition and I'm best when I solve problems independently. This means I must use trial-and-error methods in order to come up with a solution. I must also spend a lot of time reflecting on problems in order to fully grasp my solution.

My learning style does have its challenges. I'm not the best when I'm under restriction or limitations. I'm not very good with routies and I'm not great at expressing how I came to my solution, mostly because I tinker with things.

Going forward I'll have to play to my strengths by spending time by myself with a problem before reaching out for help. And though I'm not good with restrictions, I'll need to learn to maximize my time so that I don't go overboard by spending too much time on a problem.

Overall I'm glad to know what works best for me. From a growth mindset perspective, I look at this more as a chance to play to my strengths and look to others to help cover my weaknesses and perhaps improve on them. I'm glad unit 1 is done and I now look forward to the heavy coding going forward.
